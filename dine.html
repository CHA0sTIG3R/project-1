Bolero Bolero looks interesting enough to warrant going back to the busy Williamsburg thoroughfare. The casual, 50-seat Vietnamese restaurant from Matt Le-Khac and Jimmy Tran — who both used to work at fine dining restaurants like Blue Hill at Stone Barns and Benu — serves dishes such as ground wagyu beef wrapped in betel leaf, a housemade tofu with chile and crispy lemongrass, and caramel-braised pork belly, prioritizing less ubiquitous Vietnamese ingredients.
Pastrami Masters The famed brisket and pastrami of Bed-Stuy institution David’s Brisket House is now available in Williamsburg. The owners of the popular Jewish deli took over Lebanese fixture Wafa’s Express and have added pastrami alongside the shawarma.
Casa Ora Venezuelan fare gets the rare upscale treatment at Casa Ora, a project from NoMad alum Ivo Diaz, his mother Isbelis Diaz, and consulting chef Luis Herrera. Isbelis takes care of classics like arepas and empanadas, while Herrera and Ivo dish out interpretations of their favorite Venezuelan dishes.
